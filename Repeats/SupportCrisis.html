Three Types of Resources

  • Crisis and Support Lines that do not call the police
  • Warm Lines that are less likely to call the police
  • Crisis and Support Lines that are mandated reporters

Who is a Mandated Reporter and What do They Report?

  • Mandated reporters are people who work certain jobs that are required by law to report suspected abuse, neglect, and threats of harm against oneself or others to police.
  • Mandated reporters include doctors, nurses, psychitrists, social workers, councelors, psychologists, teachers, parole officers, religious leaders, firefighters, and more.
  • Mandated reporters report threats of harm (past and present) against children and vulernable populations, which includes disabled people of all ages and elderly adults.
  • You can ask any person you speak to on a crisis or support line if they are a mandated reporter and what they have to report. It is not a secret!
  • Script option: "hello, before i talk about myself, I would like to know if you are a mandated reporter and what you have to report to police."

Why do Some People Want to Avoid Mandated Reporters?

Autonomy
Because someone wants to seek help without being forced to go to the hospital against their will.
Cost
Because uninsured and underinsured callers may be foreced to pay costly medical bills from ambulance transport, emergency room visits, psychiatric holds, medications, and counseling — often court-mandated in involuntary hospitalizations.
Informed Consent.
Because most policies at crisis lines with mandated reporters mandate not informing callers when they call the police or what will happen during these police interventions.
Dislike or Distrust of Police
Because someone does not trust or has had bad expereiences with police.
Criminal History
Because someone is on parole and does not want their current crisis to be known to their parole officer.
Dislike or Distrust in the Current Legal System
Because abuse cases can be difficult to prove in our current legal system. Manated reporters are required to work in the system as it currently is, even if police or courtroom intervention is unlikely to be helpful
Dislike or Distrust of the Government
Because someone distrusts the government or systems like the Veteran's Affairs Department
Privacy
Because mandated reporters are required to track phone information in case a situation becomes necessary to report. In these cases, phone calls or texts are not truly anonymous.
LGBTQIA+ Identity
When a crisis line calls the police it risks outting that person as LGBTQIA+ which is especially dangerous for minors.
Transgender Identity
Because trangender people lack necessary legislation in all states, additional risks include being assigned to the wrong binary gender union in prisons or hospitals, gender-affirming care being witheld, and more.
Abuse
Because someone in an abusive situation may be working to leave said siutaion on their own terms but a crisis line may force that situation immediately and without consent.
Tribal Status
Because police and crisis lines are known for treating tribal citizens as if they are solely under US citizen laws which leads to issues with insurance coverage, cultural competency, and more.
Immigration Status
Because undocumented people are put at extra risk if police are called and could even risk deportation.
Past Negative Experiences
People who have had negative experiences with Mandated Reporters may avoid seeking help again due to police being called on them without their consent/

Why do Some People Use Mandated Reporter Lines?

Short Wait Times
Many mandated reporting lines are funded by the US government and are able to connect people to support in seconds.
Reccommended by medical professionals
Most doctors offices, hospitals, and pharmacies have pamphlets or crisis phone numbers of mandated reporters printed on everything from bags for presciptions to the printed after-visit summaries.
Used by Many People
Many mandated reporting lines are used by large numbers of people which many hope means that they are more effective at care or responding to crisis situations.
Local Support
Many mandated reporter lines (including some warmlines) are connected to a city or state, making them experts on local resources.
Past Postive Experiences
Many people have had positive experiences with mandated reporting phone lines and text services, including multi-marginalized people.
Uncertainty of Who to Call
When people don't know who to call to get the help they need, mandated reporter lines can be great at connecting people to other lines that specialize on their situation.
Decent Healthcare
People who have decent healthcare coverage and are considering hospitalization for feeling suicidal may find caling a Mandated Reporter helpful.
Not at risk
Many people using mandated reporter lines are not marginalized in ways that would require a mandated reporter to report them.
Non-Immigrants Without Negative Police Interactions.
Some people are not at risk of deportation, often due to ethnicity and either trust police or believe that police would only be dispatched if it truly helps them.
Calling about Others
Plenty of people who call mandated reporter lines are calling on behalf of someone else, not their own situation. These people may not even be aware of the risks involved in calling crisis lines with mandated reporters.
Lack of Informed Consent or Information.
Many people don't know the risks involved with calling or texting Mandated Reporters. While websites detail reasons Mandated Reporters involve police, that information is not transparently given to callers.

Who Should I Call?

  • The point of informed consent is enabling people to make a choice that best suits their needs.
  • Plenty of people use all 3 types of lines at different times or for different events.
  • You are in charge or how much information you choose to disclose on a call or chat. Even if you are in a situation which would require mandated reporting, if you do not tell the crisis or support line about those details, they won't be able to report you.
  • If you are calling about someone else's situation, try to call a resource that does not contact police or a warmline because you risk stripping a person of their autonomy. That said, this site is not going to tell anyone what to do.
  • Sometimes different lines will be busy or have limited hours. Please call or text multiple lines if one is busy or unavailable! You deserve to get support.
  • No matter what kind of line you call or text, ask what kind of information is collected (including location), what information they are required by law to report, what optional information they may report to police or government, and if they use informed consent before dispatching police.

Crisis and Support Lines That Do Not Call the Police

  • All of these lines do not call the police on callers experiencing suicidal thoughts or attempts.
  • NOTE: will indicate if a line files reports to police under other circumstances.
  • Many lines reserve the right to file reports on credible threats of violence, suspected child abuse or disabled adult abuse, elder abuse, or neglect.
  • Before talking about yourself, ask:
  1. if they ever operate as mandated reporters
  2. What kind of information they collect (including location and relative location)
  3. What information they are required by law to report
  4. What information they choose to report to regulatory authorities.
  5. If they use informed consent before dispatching police

Warm Lines that are Less Likely to Call the Police

  • Warm lines can be used for crisis and non-crisis situations, although many focus on non-crisis situations. Most warm lines are staffed by people who are not mandated reporters because they are peer-to-peer lines.
  • Always call resources connected to your state and county when possible. If you do not want to have a line know what state you live in, use the warmlines that accept calls or texts from anywhere in the US.
  • Many warm lines have a limit on how long someone can talk to you in order to ensure that anyone calling can be heard.
  • NOTE: These lines are more likely to call the police than the resources above. Make sure to ask what information is being reported or recorded, if anyone you are speaking to is a mandated reporter, or if this line ever sends police to callers without their knowledge or consent!

Crisis and Support Lines that are Mandated Reporters

  • If you or someone you live with are undocumented immigrants, it is important to note that police officers who respond have no hesitation in reporting undocumented immigrants to ICE.
  • These lines are known for calling the police, even if the percentage of calls directed to the police is low, that often disproportionately impacts disabled people due to mandated reporting practices which consider disabled people vulnerable adults and reporting suicidal thoughts and actions.
  • Mandated reporters are specifically trained with crisis in mind, which some people prefer because it is more likely that someone is connected to an expereienced person.
