Goals of this site

This site has been created to:

  • Provide resources about disabilities by and for disabled people/people with disabilities
  • Address the lack of support for marginalized disabled resources in the colonized country of America.
  • Intentionally center Black and Indigneous identites.
  • Balance the need of both medical and community based resources.
  • Address common questions related to the risks and benefits of getting a disabiltiy diagnosis while simultaneously supporting self-diagnosis.
  • Empower disabled people/people with disabilties to self-advocate in all areas of life, including at a doctor's office.
  • Help disabled people/People with disabilities access disability community, disability pride, disability history, and disability justice.
  • Validate disabled people/people with disabilites who have had negative experienes with medical providers like doctors or systems like the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA)
  • Counter the historical and continuing harmful medical idea that all disability is binary, meant to be cured, and easily cured.
  • Make information that doesn't center the medical institution but instead centers disabled identities expressing our lives in community.

To establish credibility, resources continue to be collected over a decade of:

  • Lived experience as a multi-disabled person affected by both mental and physical disabilies.
  • Doing detailed research after both negative and positive expereinces with doctors.
  • Lived experience as an Indigenous person.
  • Almost a decade of work as a community disability advocate which includes having conversations with people from all walks of life and different stages of disability acceptance and status.
  • Disability studies courses and personal study.
  • Medical experience as a patient.
  • Medical research as a hobby as the flawed field continues to grow and evolve.
  • Genuine care for qualified information that utilizes fact-checking from a Library Science perspective and lived experience from varying communties.
  • A continued personal and community based investment in disability justice, disability pride, and disability community.
